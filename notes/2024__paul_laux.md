# A Dynamic Bayesian Network Approach to Interbank Market

_2024 · Paul Laux (University of Delaware)_

**Headline:** This talk presents a dynamic Bayesian network model for interbank overnight funding networks, combining economic factors with Bayesian estimation to predict counterparty choices and pricing.

## Key findings
- The model validates that simple economics in a dynamic setting can predict counterparty choices and pricing usefully.  
  > The results validate the simple dynamic network understanding-it is enough to be useful
- Lending-leaning banks tend not to lend to borrowing-leaning banks (disassortative tendencies).  
  > Negative β₀s. Lending-leaning banks tend NOT to lend to borrowing-leaning banks, and vice versa
- Relationships are more persistent during stressful periods (crises).  
  > History matters, especially in stressful times
