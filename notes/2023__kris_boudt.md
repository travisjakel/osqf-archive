## Key findings
- MPC for positive income changes is ~9% vs ~6.5% for negative changes, showing household resiliency  
  > MPC for positive income changes is around 9%... households resiliency
- Semi-durable consumption has twice the MPC of average (18% for gains, 13% for losses)  
  > MPC for semi-durables is twice the average MPC: 18% vs 9%... 13% vs 6.5%
- MPC varies by income change type: large negative transient changes show 0.08-0.20 elasticity  
  > m_i,t × large negative transient change | 0.08 | 0.20 | 0.11 | 0.09
- Wealthier households have lower MPC, but non-durable consumption remains stable across income changes  
  > MPC is highest for households with lower wealth... Non-durable consumption is very stable
