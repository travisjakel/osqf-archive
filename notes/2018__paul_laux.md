# The Timing of Variance Risk Premia around Macroeconomic News Events

_2018 · Paul Laux (University of Delaware)_

**Headline:** The study shows that costs of insuring against variance and jump risk are concentrated around macroeconomic news events, with significant returns around specific announcements like Non-Farm Payrolls and FOMC meetings.

## Key findings
- Realized returns around non-announcement days are about zero  
  > Realized returns around non-announcement days are about zero
- Positive costs of insuring against variance risk increases around statistical announcements  
  > We measure positive cost of insuring against variance risk increases around statistical announcement (employment, CPI, etc.), where risk is often heightened
- Positive costs of insuring against jump risk decreases around FOMC meetings  
  > We measure positive costs of insuring against jump risk decreases around FOMC meetings, where risk is often reduced
- Negative returns for delta-neutral SPX straddles around Non-Farm Payrolls  
  > Non-farm Payrolls: -2.4 percent per day for delta-neutral SPX straddles
