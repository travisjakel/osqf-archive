## Key findings
- Random errors in frequentist pricing models create exploitable return patterns (size, value, low volatility).  
  > If prices are formed by frequentists, random errors cause exploitable return patterns including by size, price to book, and low volatility
- Bayesian adjustments eliminate exploitable errors by aligning prices with the common mean.  
  > KEY PROPERTIES OF CORRECTLY ADJUSTED PRICES... errors are now uncorrelated with market values, not intrinsic values
- Historical data shows pricing errors exist alongside frequentist pricing mechanisms.  
  > The historical record suggests the presence of pricing errors as well as prices that are more frequentist than Bayesian
