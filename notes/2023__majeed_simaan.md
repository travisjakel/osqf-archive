## Key findings
- A BTD policy MAY enhance the terminal wealth of the investor over time, subject to market conditions and dip measurement.  
  > A BTD policy MAY enhance the terminal wealth of the investor over time, subject to several factors:
- BTD strategies show higher risk-adjusted returns (higher Sortino ratio) during market reversals but underperform in terminal wealth compared to benchmarks.  
  > A BTD strategy weakly underperforms an optimally balanced benchmark... lower terminal wealth
- Using maximum drawdown (MDD) to measure dips improves risk-adjusted performance.  
  > There is much potential improvement of risk-adjusted performance when investors use maximum drawdown (MDD) to measure potential dip.
