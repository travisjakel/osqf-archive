## Key findings
- Taxable account optimal stock fraction (f*) is higher than Roth IRA due to higher capital loss tax rates.  
  > It’s because the capital loss rate, τl = 0.28, is greater than the capital gain rate, τg = 0.15!
- When investor dies at liquidation, optimal interval width (∆f) increases by 17% to defer gains.  
  > Taxable account (investor dies at T ): f ∗ = 0.76 ∆f = 0.17.
- Increasing capital loss tax rate (τl) increases optimal f* but decreases ∆f to generate more losses.  
  > Increase f ∗ : As the capital loss tax rate, τl , increases, stock becomes more desirable...
- Higher capital gains tax rate (τg) decreases optimal f* but increases ∆f to reduce realized gains.  
  > Not surprising: As τg increases, ∆f increases to reduce realizing capital gains.
- Using average cost basis instead of full cost basis reduces utility by ~1% in some scenarios.  
  > We use the average cost basis... quantitatively measure of the suboptimality generated by the average cost basis.
