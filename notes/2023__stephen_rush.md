# Non-Standard Errors

_2023 · Stephen Rush_

**Headline:** The study introduces non-standard errors (NSE), defined as the inter-quartile range of estimates from different researchers testing the same hypotheses on the same sample, and examines their magnitude and determinants in finance.

## Key findings
- NSEs are the same order of magnitude as standard errors  
  > NSEs are the same order of magnitude as standard errors
- Peer feedback reduces NSEs by 19.4-26.4% depending on factors  
  > NSEs decline significantly after peer-feedback
- +1 SD in academic seniority reduces NSE by 19.4%  
  > +1 SD in academic seniority reduces NSE by 19.4%
- Team size reductions correlate with IQR decreases  
  > +1 SD in team size reduces IQR by 12.5%
- NSEs are underestimated by participants  
  > They are underestimated by participants
