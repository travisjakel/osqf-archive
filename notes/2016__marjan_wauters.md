## Key findings
- Low-risk portfolios show higher average returns, lower risk, lower gap risk, and lower turnover compared to market-cap portfolios.  
  > Low-risk: higher average return, lower risk (in spite of higher exposure to equity portfolio), lower gap risk, lower turnover: stability gains
- CPPI performance differences are amplified in bearish markets when protection is most needed.  
  > Differences are amplified in bearish markets i.e., when protection is most needed
- Low-risk equity portfolios achieve 0% gap risk in bullish markets, unlike market-cap portfolios.  
  > Low-risk: ... 0 gaps in bull market
