_2018 · Matthew Ginley_

**Headline:** The talk presents methods for rare events forecasting using kernel density estimators and monotonic constraints, applied to corporate bankruptcy prediction.

## Key findings
- Monotonicity can be thought of as a 'relaxation' of linearity. Very useful!  
  > Monotonicity can be thought of as a 'relaxation' of linearity. Very useful!
- Approximation using KDE and monotonic constraints enables effective modeling of rare events like bankruptcy.  
  > We can approximate the information contained in the data and then exploit the presence of monotonicity to minimize what we might have lost
- The method captures non-linear dynamics and excels in out-of-sample extrapolation for high-risk scenarios.  
  > effectively capture non-linear dynamics... shines with out of sample extrapolation in the extremes corresponding with high risk
