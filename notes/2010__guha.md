## Key findings
- .Rdata format is poor for large/many objects  
  > .Rdata format is poor for large/many objects
- Hadoop DFS stores files as blocks across a cluster  
  > Files are divided into blocks, replicated and stored across the cluster
- MapReduce enables gigabyte-scale computations in R  
  > Can do much more, works on gigabytes of data and across computers
- RHIPE can save complex R objects (data frames, factors) to HDFS  
  > Can write and save a variety of R objects - Atomic vectors,lists and attributes - ... data frames, factors etc.
- Factorial design experiments scaled from 80 days to 6.5 hours with 320 cores  
  > Across 320 cores(EC2 cluster, 80 c1.medium instances): 6.5 hrs ($100)
