# The informational role of algorithmic traders in the option market

_2015 · Rohini Grover (IGIDR Finance Research Group)_

**Headline:** Examines whether algorithmic traders generate informed trading in index options, finding no predictive power in their net buying pressure (NBP).

## Key findings
- Direction-learning and volatility-learning hypotheses do not hold for algorithmic traders (AT) in aggregate or subgroups.  
  > ■ The direction-learning and volatility-learning hypotheses do not hold for AT: neither in aggregate, nor for any specific investor sub-group within AT.
- AT's NBP has no predictive power for future index returns.  
  > ■ NBP of AT has no prediction power for future index returns.
- Non-AT directional trading shows significant direction-learning effects.  
  > ■ The direction-learning hypothesis holds for non-AT in aggregate as well as for each investor subgroup.
