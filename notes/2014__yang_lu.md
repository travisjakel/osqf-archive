**Headline:** Re-evaluating the low-risk anomaly in finance using matching techniques to address portfolio imbalances

## Code & tools

## Key findings
- Original low beta portfolios outperformed high beta portfolios by 10.5% vs 4.6% annually  
  > Low beta portfolios (10.5% p.a.) outperform high beta portfolios (4.6% p.a.)
- Matching reduced the anomaly magnitude to 7.9% vs 4.9% annually  
  > Lowest Beta Quintile 10.5 7.9 Highest Beta Quintile 4.6 4.9
- Imbalance between quintiles contributed to the original anomaly  
  > Part of the low beta anomaly observed is due to imbalance between high and low beta quintile portfolios
- High beta portfolios outperformed low beta portfolios during Oct '99-Jun '00  
  > High beta quintile portfolio outperformed low beta quintile portfolio cumulatively from Oct. '99 through June '00
