## Key findings
- Private equity returns can be decomposed into tradable factors (β) and non-spanned alpha (α)  
  > Returns can be decomposed into a component that can be replicated by traded factors (β) and an additional return premium (α) that is not spanned by publicly traded factors
- Aggregate private equity segment risks can be fully hedged using public assets  
  > The returns of the entire private-equity segment are fully spanned by traded assets, and the risks can be perfectly hedged by dynamically trading the public assets
- Alpha persists in private equity due to manager-specific factors  
  > Under full spanning, the risk of the private-equity segment is traded in the market, but the private-equity segment can still earn a positive alpha
