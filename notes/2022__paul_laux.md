_2022 · Paul Laux (University of Delaware)_

## Key findings
- Banks broaden lending connections during stress ('counterparty sampling') to improve market-wide information about future interest rates  
  > We provide evidence that counterparty sampling improves market-wide information about future interest rates
- Liquidity disruptions in Italian tax transfer periods caused significant increases in interest rate volatility  
  > Interest rate volatility high in PTTP (pre tax transfer periods, uncertainty) vs. TTP (tax transfer periods, resolution)
- Network measures like entropy and clique size showed significant changes during stress periods  
  > Entropy 0.574 (PTTP) vs 0.840 (TTP), z=2.821
