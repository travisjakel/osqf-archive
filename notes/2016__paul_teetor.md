# Are You Trading Mean Reversion or Oscillation?

_2016 · Paul Teetor (William Blair)_

**Headline:** The talk argues that markets oscillate rather than mean-revert, highlighting the limitations of the ADF test in identifying tradable opportunities.

## Key findings
- Markets alternate between mean diverting and mean reverting  
  > Markets alternate between mean diverting and mean reverting.
- ADF test may mislead by indicating non-mean reversion when markets are oscillating  
  > If market oscillates, repeated mean diversion forces conclusion that market is not mean reverting. · Confuses traders (mistakenly) searching for mean reversion.
- Level-slope regression ranks oscillation candidates but requires human judgment  
  > The level-slope regression test can rank possibilities, but human judgement is ultimately required.
