# How much structure is best

_2017 · Andy Tang (William Blair)_

**Headline:** The talk explores covariance matrix estimation techniques, comparing fully unstructured methods (sample, shrinkage, EWMA) with conditionally structured approaches (region/sector effects) and their impact on forecast accuracy.

## Key findings
- Fully unstructured methods include sample, shrinkage, and EWMA.  
  > Examples: Sample, Shrinkage, EWMA
- Fully unstructured approaches face curse of dimensionality and misspecification errors.  
  > Curse of dimensionality! Misspecification error!
- Conditionally structured methods use region/sector effects during crises.  
  > Method 3: Region effects during 'European Debt Crisis'
- Optimal structure balance improves forecast accuracy.  
  > Conclusion: Structureness? not too much, not too little, just in the middle!
