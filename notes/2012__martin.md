# Robust Statistics in Finance

_2012 · Martin (University of Washington)_

**Headline:** This talk explains how robust statistical methods improve portfolio construction by reducing sensitivity to outliers compared to classical techniques like least squares regression.

## Key findings
- Classical beta estimates can be highly misleading due to outliers (e.g., 3.17 vs robust 1.13)  
  > CLASSICAL BETA = 3.17... predicts future betas poorly!
- Robust methods provide more stable relationships in size-return studies  
  > Positive relationship is rather constant... even at 1% trimming
- Robust regression reduces bias in factor models  
  > Reduced bias and variability of exposures estimates... more accurate robust t-statistics
