## Key findings
- Quantile regression minimizes asymmetric linear loss, generalizing to regression settings  
  > Regression Quantiles also minimize asymmetric linear loss Optimization generalizes nicely to the regression setting
- Quantile regression provides conditional quantile estimates for heterogeneous relationships  
  > the conditional τth quantile solves ατ(x) = min EY|X=x ρτ(Y − q(x))
- Engel curves show varying elasticity of food expenditure across income quantiles  
  > Seven estimated quantile regression lines for τ ∈ {.05, .1, .25, .5, .75, .9, .95}
