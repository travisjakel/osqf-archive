# On the Maximization of Sharpe Ratio

_2012 · Pav (Cerebellum Capital)_

**Headline:** The talk explores statistical methods for maximizing the Sharpe Ratio, including t-statistics, Hotelling tests, and sample size considerations.

## Key findings
- The Sharpe ratio is equivalent to a scaled t-statistic  
  > Sharpe ratio is, up to scaling, the t-statistic
- Sample size needed for a given SNR follows n ≈ κ/ζ²  
  > n ≈ κ/ζ² with κ a function of type I and type II rates
- Hotelling T² generalizes Sharpe ratio to multivariate portfolios  
  > Hotelling T² is multivariate generalization of Student’s t
- Overfitting strategies show inflated in-sample Sharpe ratios  
  > MLE of ζ∗ is 0.1yr−1/2 with 95% CI [0, 1.3]
