## Key findings
- Current 95% VaR increases are driven by exposure changes, not covariance
- MCMC enables tail risk modeling with synthesized data matching joint probability distributions
- Kolmogorov-Smirnov tests validate MCMC-generated distributions (p-values ~65%)
- Stress-testing should complement traditional risk measures with creative scenario analysis
- Rising tail risk (difference between 99% & 95% VaR) indicates increasing event risk probability
